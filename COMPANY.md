## Public repository policy

We publish selected open-source tools, reusable components, documentation, demonstrations, and sanitised examples. We do not publish:

- Client-owned or client-confidential source code
- Production credentials, API keys, environment variables, or infrastructure secrets
- Personally identifiable information or customer datasets
- Proprietary internal systems without a deliberate release review
- Claims, metrics, certifications, or customer names that cannot be publicly verified

Public examples should use sample or synthetic data, remove private identifiers, and clearly communicate limitations.

## Engineering principles

1. **Security by design** — secrets, access control, privacy, and dependency risk are considered before release.
2. **Production-minded delivery** — architecture, testing, documentation, deployment, and observability are part of the product.
3. **Clear ownership** — client intellectual property and confidential repositories remain protected.
4. **Responsible AI** — outputs should identify assumptions, limitations, and areas requiring human review.
5. **No invented proof** — public materials must not use fake customers, fabricated metrics, or unsupported guarantees.
6. **Open collaboration** — useful generic tools may be shared when doing so is secure, lawful, and beneficial.
